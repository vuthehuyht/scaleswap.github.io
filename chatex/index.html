What is KYC?

KYC – know your customer (know your customer), is an international normative act by which a customer is identified. The general meaning of KYC is to provide the organization that conducts financial transactions with a package of documents that contain information about the client. Of course, such rules at one time met with a stream of criticism from the cryptocurrency community, but now this practice is welcomed by many large cryptocurrency players, trying to protect their clients from hacking their wallets and conducting illegal transactions on sites.

What data is required?

Chatex collects information from its users such as contact information: name, home address, email address, date and place of birth, mobile phone number, and Chatex also collects account information such as username and password. In addition, to undergo the KYC procedure, you need:

  • Financial information such as bank account numbers, bank statements, and trade information.
  • Identity verification information, such as a picture of your government ID, passport, national ID or driver’s license, and under special conditions Social Security number (for US residents).
  • Information to prove your place of residence, such as a utility bill, telephone bill, or similar document.

This package of documents provides an opportunity to protect our users from fraudulent actions by scammers and other people encroaching on your assets. In addition, transparent verification will help you trade large volumes on our platform, as well as seamlessly manage your cryptocurrency funds.

 

Why you shouldn’t worry about the safety of your data?

The KYC procedure is carried out in accordance with all security measures, which means that your data will not leak into the network and will not be used by advertising agencies and people trying to get your data illegally.
